# Vertical Applications of SnapSpace

SnapSpace applies a consistent execution model across a variety of industries that demand reliability and auditability for irreversible actions. Supporting multiple verticals matters: it demonstrates the substrate’s flexibility and the universal need for safe, deterministic execution in high-stakes domains.

At the core across all verticals is the invariant of exactly-once execution under uncertain or adversarial conditions, preventing duplication, loss, and unintended consequences even when inputs are nondeterministic.

## Safety / Alerting

Timely safety alerts and incident notifications must reach endpoints exactly once—neither missed nor duplicated—regardless of network state or system failures.

## Payments / Billing

Transaction and settlement engines require atomicity and finality for financial operations. SnapSpace enforces deterministic outcomes for transfers and reconciliations.

## Trading / Finance

In finance, order execution must be resilient to errors, double-processing, and uncertain conditions. SnapSpace provides an auditable, deterministic execution path.

## AI Agent Execution Governance

When AI agents take real-world actions, such as provisioning resources or making decisions, their reasoning (probabilistic) is separated from action (deterministic commit). SnapSpace governs the “point of no return” for such execution.

## Infrastructure Automation

Automation tools must execute critical infrastructure changes exactly once. SnapSpace provides a boundary that guarantees action integrity and recoverability.

## Robotics

Autonomous systems and robots often operate in unpredictable environments. SnapSpace ensures that life-critical operations, such as actuator commands, are executed in a deterministic and safe manner.

## Driverless Vehicles / Autonomous Mobility

In autonomous mobility, real-world interventions—like braking, steering, or route changes—must be made exactly once, regardless of upstream sensor or control uncertainty.

---

SnapSpace’s approach unifies these verticals under a single, verifiable execution model.
